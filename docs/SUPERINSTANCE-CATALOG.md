### Testable Predictions (15+)

From the three frameworks (Dials Not Laws, Innovation Cycle, Conservation of Tension):

#### From Dials Not Laws
1. The 82% unexplored parameter space contains at least 3 viable new musical scales
2. Continuous parameter adjustment will outperform categorical tuning systems in listener preference
3. Cross-cultural music traditions will cluster around the same 5 dial regions
4. Neural imaging will show graded (not categorical) responses to consonance variation
5. Composers working with continuous dials will produce more diverse output than those using discrete scales

#### From the Innovation Cycle
6. Each of the 6 innovation phases will show distinct computational signatures
7. Technology acceleration will compress the cycle duration by ~40% per decade
8. Musical constraint systems follow the same innovation dynamics as engineering constraints
9. The current phase (computational exploration) will peak within 5 years
10. Phase transitions are detectable in publication and patent data

#### From Conservation of Tension (Hypothesis)
11. If conservation holds, total tension across all voices in a piece will remain approximately constant over time
12. Conservation will be stronger in composed music than improvisation
13. The conservation coefficient will correlate with genre (strictest → classical, loosest → free jazz)
14. Breaking conservation will produce perceptible "tension violations" detectable by listeners
15. Conservation strength will vary across cultures predictably by musical tradition complexity

#### Cross-Framework
