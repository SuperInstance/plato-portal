## External Resources — Mathematical Prerequisites

These are the foundational concepts the ecosystem builds upon. If you want to go deeper, start here.

### Laman Rigidity Theory
> The mathematical framework for understanding when a structure is rigid — the basis for constraint independence.
- 📖 [Laman graph — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laman_graph) — Introduction to Laman graphs and rigidity
- 📄 [Rigidity Theory — Graver, Servatius, Servatius (book)](https://www.springer.com/gp/book/9780817631386) — Comprehensive textbook
- 📺 [Rigidity Theory: A Survey (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1203.4130) — Academic survey paper

### Eisenstein Integers
> The complex integers that form hexagonal lattices — the natural home for constraint theory's number theory.
- 📖 [Eisenstein integer — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eisenstein_integer) — Definition and properties
- 📖 [Eisenstein prime — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eisenstein_prime) — Primes in the Eisenstein ring
- 📄 [Quadratic Integer — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadratic_integer) — Broader context

### Kuramoto Model (Synchronization)
> How coupled oscillators spontaneously synchronize — the physics behind fleet resonance.
- 📖 [Kuramoto model — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuramoto_model) — Complete introduction
- 📺 [Strogatz: From Kuramoto to Crawford (SIAM Review)](https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/S0036144503425047) — Classic review article
- 📖 [Sync — Steven Strogatz (book)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/49298.Sync) — Accessible book on synchronization

### Persistent Homology & Betti Numbers
> The topology of data — how to measure the shape of constraint manifolds.
- 📖 [Persistent homology — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persistent_homology) — Introduction
- 📖 [Betti number — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betti_number) — Definition and meaning
- 📄 [Computing Persistent Homology (Zomorodian & Carlsson)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00454-004-1146-y) — Foundational algorithm paper
- 📺 [A User's Guide to Topological Data Analysis (Carlsson)](https://www.ams.org/journals/bull/2009-46-02/S0273-0979-09-01249-X/) — Bulletin of the AMS survey

### Lyapunov Exponents
> Measuring chaos — how to tell if a constraint system is stable or divergent.
- 📖 [Lyapunov exponent — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyapunov_exponent) — Definition and computation
- 📄 [Chaos and Lyapunov Exponents (review)](https://www.scholarpedia.org/article/Lyapunov_exponent) — Scholarpedia in-depth article

### Chinese Sanfen Sunyi (三分损益)
> The ancient Chinese method of generating musical pitches by adding/subtracting thirds — a constraint system millennia old.
- 📖 [三分损益法 — Wikipedia (Chinese)](https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E4%B8%89%E5%88%86%E6%90%9F%E7%9B%8A%E6%B3%95) — Chinese Wikipedia article
- 📖 [Shi Er Lü — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shi_Er_L%C3%BC) — The 12 pitches of the Chinese system
- 📄 [Chinese Music Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_musicology) — Wikipedia overview of Chinese musicology

### Indian Raga Theory
> Raga as constraint profile — the world's most sophisticated melodic constraint system.
- 📖 [Raga — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raga) — Introduction to raga
- 📖 [Śruti (music) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%9Aruti_(music)) — The 22 microtonal divisions
- 📖 [Tala (music) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tala_(music)) — Rhythmic constraint cycles

### Arabic Maqam Theory
> Maqam as constraint manifold — quarter-tone scales and modulation pathways.
- 📖 [Arabic maqam — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_maqam) — Introduction to maqam

### MIDI Specification
> The digital protocol that makes constraint music playable.
- 📖 [MIDI — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIDI) — Overview

> The language of constraints made formal — functors, comonads, and Galois connections.
- 📖 [Category theory — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Category_theory) — Overview
