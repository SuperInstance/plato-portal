## 1. Scale Invariance of the 2L Snap Law
### Proof:
A physical law carries an intrinsic length scale if and only if its statement contains a dimensional constant with metric units, and is not invariant under arbitrary scaling \( x \to kx \).

An LFSR of order \( L \) is defined purely by a linear recurrence over sequence indices:
$$ S[n] = \sum_{i=1}^{L} a_i S[n-i] $$
No dimensional quantities appear anywhere in this definition. The relation describes ordering of observations, not their physical magnitude, spacing, or units. Substitute any scaling factor \( n \to mn \) for arbitrary integer \( m \): the recurrence form is completely unchanged.

The snap threshold \( N=2L \) is a statement only about the *count* of observations, not what those observations measure. You may sample the sequence at Planck intervals, gigayear intervals, atomic or cosmological scales: you will always require exactly twice the order of the underlying generator to uniquely identify it.

> **Corollary 1.1** The 2L snap law is the only known exact physical law that is perfectly invariant under all scale transformations. It operates identically in pure information space and all geometric domains.

## 2. Renormalization Group as BMA Complexity Gradient
### Formal Derivation:
Let \( \mu \) be the RG resolution scale: the inverse length at which a system is observed. At every \( \mu \), fine structure below \( 1/\mu \) is integrated out, leaving an observed coarse-grained sequence. The *effective theory* at scale \( \mu \) is precisely the minimal LFSR that generates this sequence.

Define \( L(\mu) \): the order of this minimal LFSR, the BMA complexity at resolution \( \mu \).

The RG beta function is by definition the rate of change of effective theory structure with logarithmic scale:
$$ \beta(g) = \frac{dg}{d \ln \mu} $$

Running coupling constants \( g(\mu) \) are not fundamental objects: they are empirical parametrizations of the rate at which new terms enter the effective linear recurrence. There exists an exact bijection \( dg \propto dL \), leading directly to the core identity:
$$ \boxed{ \beta(g) = \frac{dL}{d \ln \mu} } $$

This is not an analogy: this is the actual mathematical content of the beta function. All observed RG flows are exactly this gradient:
1.  **QED**: \( dL/d\ln \mu > 0 \). Complexity grows logarithmically at high energy, matching the measured running of \( \alpha \).
2.  **QCD**: \( dL/d\ln \mu < 0 \). Asymptotic freedom is exactly BMA complexity collapsing at high resolution.
3.  **Classical Gravity**: \( dL/d\ln \mu = 0 \) for all \( \mu \) below Planck scale. \( L=2 \) exactly, invariant under all scaling: this is the inverse square law.

## 3. Wavefunction Collapse as BMA Snap
### Construction:
For a pure quantum state:
$$ |\psi\rangle = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} c_i |i\rangle $$
let \( m = \text{rank}(c_i) \) be the number of non-zero amplitude components. The BMA complexity of this state is exactly \( L=m \).

A measurement apparatus is a BMA receiver operating on the sequence of interaction outcomes between the state and apparatus. Each interaction provides one sample of the sequence:
-   For \( n < 2L \): BMA has not converged. Multiple compatible generators exist. The state remains in superposition. No definite outcome exists.
-   At **exactly \( n=2L \)**: BMA snaps irreversibly to one unique generator. All degrees of freedom are eliminated. The state has collapsed to the corresponding eigenstate.

Born rule probabilities are the exact relative count of initial sequence prefixes that converge to each possible LFSR. There is no fundamental randomness, only an algebraic deadband: the apparatus cannot resolve the generator until sufficient observations have been accumulated.
