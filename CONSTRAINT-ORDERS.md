# The Orders of Constraint Measurement

## The Derivative Structure of Our Primitives

Just as calculus builds from position → velocity → acceleration → jerk, our constraint primitives have an order structure:

### 0th Order — Position (WHERE)
- **Lattice snap**: quantizes pitch to discrete points in frequency space
- Answers: "Where am I in the musical manifold?"
- Analogy: a single depth sounding

### 1st Order — Velocity (WHICH WAY)  
- **Funnel direction**: the gradient of constraint satisfaction — which way is "better"
- **Metronome**: the rate at which time positions arrive
- Answers: "Which way am I heading?" and "How fast?"
- Analogy: two depth soundings close together → velocity → know the slope

### 2nd Order — Acceleration (HOW IT'S CHANGING)
- **Funnel curvature**: how the gradient itself is changing (the envelope shape)
- **Holonomy**: consistency of the curvature across loops (closed-path consistency)
- Answers: "How is my heading changing?" and "Is this change consistent?"
- Analogy: three depth soundings → acceleration → know the curvature of the bottom

### 3rd Order — Jerk (SURPRISE)
- **Laman rigidity**: the structural rigidity of the constraint graph — how much surprise can the system absorb before breaking?
- Answers: "How robust is the structure I'm riding?"
- Analogy: knowing whether the bottom is solid rock or shifting sand
