## First: Formalize the core property
First let's state clearly the unstated mathematical property here. The observation relies on the foundational geometric fact:
> The total degrees of freedom of a rigid body (translation + rotation) in d dimensional Euclidean space is exactly the triangular number:
>  $$ \dim(\text{SE}(d)) = \frac{d(d+1)}{2} = T_d$$
The property being investigated is: *find the smallest integer N such that N is divisible by every T_d for d in some set of relevant dimensions*. This is a perfectly well defined LCM problem, not woo.

## 1. Is 360 the smallest number with this property?
### First: full unfiltered case (all d from 1 to 9)
Compute LCM of all triangular numbers up to d=9:
|d | T_d | prime factors |
|---|---|---|
|1 | 1 | - |
|2 | 3 | 3 |
|3 | 6 | 2×3 |
|4 | 10 | 2×5 |
|5 | 15 | 3×5 |
|6 | 21 | 3×7 |
|7 | 28 | 2²×7 |
|8 | 36 | 2²×3² |
|9 | 45 | 3²×5 |
$$ \text{LCM}(1,3,6,10,15,21,28,36,45) = 2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5 \times 7 = 1260 $$
> **Critical observation**: The original argument *intentionally omitted d=6 and d=7*. This is the only reason it landed on 360 instead of 1260.
### Now for the restricted set given {1,2,3,4,5,8,9,15}:
Compute LCM of their T_d values:
$$ \text{LCM}(1,3,6,10,15,36,45,120) = 360 $$
Checking the candidates:
- 120: fails on T₈=36, T₉=45
- 180: fails on T₁₅=120
- 240: fails on T₉=45
- 300: fails on T₈=36
✅ **360 is indeed strictly the minimum integer for this exact set of dimensions**. There is no smaller number that works.
