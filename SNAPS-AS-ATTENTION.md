## The Poker Player

A poker player doesn't calculate odds. They can't — there isn't time, and the calculation isn't the point.

What they do:

1. **Feel the delta from average.** The river card flips. They don't compute P(win). They feel the SHIFT — "this changed things" or "this didn't." The felt delta IS the information. Not the probability. The CHANGE in probability relative to expectation.

2. **Watch the delta in others.** Not the other players' cards — the other players' REACTIONS. The micro-delta in their behavior. A twitch, a pause, a change in breathing. The delta between "how they normally act" and "how they're acting now."

3. **Understand if their odds improved.** Not by computing. By feeling whether the cumulative deltas (the river + the reactions + the betting pattern) land on the "good" side or "bad" side of their internal snap function.

The poker player is running constraint theory in real-time:
- The snap = "close enough to my expected pattern that I can ignore it"
- The felt delta = "far enough from expected that I need to PAY ATTENTION"
- The attention allocation = "of all the deltas I felt, which ones can I actually DO something about?"

**The snap doesn't tell you what's true. The snap tells you what you can SAFELY IGNORE so you can think about what matters.**

## Snaps as Compression of Relevance

### The Problem Every Mind Faces

There's too much information. Always. A human brain processes ~11 million bits/s of sensory input but is conscious of ~50 bits/s. That's a 220,000:1 compression ratio.

How? **The snap function.**

Everything "close enough to expected" gets snapped to "expected." It doesn't reach consciousness. Only the deltas — the things that DON'T snap — get attention.

### The Snap Is the Gatekeeper of Attention

```
Incoming information
    ↓
Snap function (tolerance-based)
    ├── Within tolerance → SNAP to "expected" → ignore (background)
    └── Outside tolerance → DELTA detected → attend (foreground)
        ↓
    What can thinking affect here?
        ├── Can affect → FOCUS (allocate cognition)
        └── Can't affect → ACCEPT (update snap baseline)
```

The snap function determines what reaches consciousness. Everything within tolerance is compressed away. Only the deltas survive.

### This Is Why Tolerance Matters

Too tight a tolerance:
- Everything triggers as a delta
- Attention floods, overload, paralysis
- Can't focus on anything because everything demands attention
- This is anxiety. This is sensory overload. This is the autistic experience of raw sensation.

Too loose a tolerance:
- Nothing triggers as a delta
- Everything snaps to "expected"
- Miss genuine anomalies, dangers, opportunities
- Can't learn because nothing is surprising
- This is complacency. This is inattention. This is the experienced driver who stops seeing the road.

**The right tolerance is the tolerance that compresses exactly the right amount of context.** Enough that the mind can focus. Not so much that it misses what matters.

## Platonic Solids as Flavors of Randomness

### What Casey Means

The Platonic solids aren't geometric objects in this context. They're **archetypes of how uncertainty can be structured.**

Think of it this way: randomness isn't one thing. A coin flip has a different FLAVOR of randomness than a dice roll, which has a different flavor than drawing from a shuffled deck, which has a different flavor than the stock market.

Each flavor of randomness has a different structure:
- **Coin flip**: binary (2 outcomes) — tetrahedral structure (2 vertices ± choice)
- **Dice roll**: uniform (6 outcomes) — cubic structure (6 faces)
- **Card draw**: combinatorial (52 choose 5) — high-dimensional structure
- **Stock market**: correlated + heavy-tailed — complex structure with hidden regularities

The "Platonic solid" of a random process is the shape of its possibility space — the geometry that makes some outcomes snap together and others stand apart.

### The 5 Flavors

| Flavor | Randomness type | What snaps together | What stands out |
|--------|----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Tetrahedral | Binary/categorical | Same category | Different category |
| Cubic | Uniform/independent | Same magnitude | Outlier magnitude |
| Octahedral | Directional (8 compass points) | Same direction | Wrong direction |
| Dodecahedral | Rich combinatorial (20) | Nearby combinations | Distant combinations |
| Icosahedral | Dense cluster (12 directions, φ spacing) | Golden-ratio clusters | Non-golden anomalies |

Each flavor has a different snap topology — a different way of saying "these things are close enough to treat as the same."

### The Poker Game Uses Multiple Flavors Simultaneously

The poker player processes:
- **Card probabilities** (cubic/uniform randomness)
- **Player behavior** (categorical randomness — "tight" vs "loose" vs "tilting")
- **Betting patterns** (directional randomness — aggressive vs passive)
- **Emotional state** (rich combinatorial — micro-expressions, timing, speech)
- **Table dynamics** (cluster randomness — who's in conflict with whom)

Each layer has its own flavor of randomness, its own snap function, its own tolerance. The experienced player has well-calibrated snaps at every layer.

**The beginner's problem isn't that they can't calculate. It's that their snaps are miscalibrated.** They attend to the wrong things (the cards) and miss the right things (the deltas in the players).

## The Rubik's Cube: Scripts Free the Mind

### God's Number vs Human Play

God's number for a 3×3 Rubik's cube is 20 moves — every scrambled state can be solved in 20 or fewer moves. Yet speedcubers routinely execute 100-150 moves in a solve.

Why? Because the optimal path is not the point. **The point is the path the mind can actually traverse.**

The cube has 6 faces, each with 3 rotational snaps (quarter turns). That's 18 possible moves per step. The permutation space is 43 quintillion. No human brute-forces this. Not even close.

### What Speedcubers Actually Do

They learn **groups of sequences** — chunks of moves that produce known transformations. These chunks become a vocabulary:

```
OLL (orient last layer):   57 algorithms, each 6-12 moves
PLL (permute last layer):  21 algorithms, each 6-16 moves  
F2L (first two layers):    ~100 pattern-response pairs
```

Each algorithm is a SCRIPT. The cuber doesn't think through the individual moves. They see a pattern, recognize it (snap!), and execute the associated script on autopilot.

The scripts run WITHOUT THINKING. That's the point. The scripts are offloaded cognition. While the hands execute the current script, the eyes and mind are already scanning for the NEXT pattern — planning 2-3 scripts ahead.

### The Grammar of Oscillations

The scripts aren't random. They have a GRAMMAR — rules about which scripts compose, which sequences chain, which states transition to which other states. The cuber has internalized this grammar. It's their own learned language.

Could they express it completely? Could they transfer it? Mostly yes — it's written down in algorithm tables. But the FEEL of when to deploy which script, the rhythm of the composition, the sense of "I'm three scripts from solved" — that's the part that's embodied. The vocabulary transfers. The grammar partially transfers. The feel is personal.

### The Real Intelligence

**The intelligence is NOT the scripts.** The scripts are now just procedures to be followed.

**The intelligence is: knowing when to think.**

```
See pattern → snap to known script → execute WITHOUT THINKING
                                    ↓
                        While executing, mind is FREE
                                    ↓
                Eyes scan for next pattern → plan next script
                                    ↓
                        Compose scripts into strategy
                                    ↓
                    "I'm three scripts from solved"
```

The snap does the critical work: **"This is a known pattern → I don't need to think about it."** The snap frees the mind. The freed mind plans ahead. The plan is the intelligence.

Without the snap, the mind would be bogged down computing each move. With the snap, the mind is free to compose at a higher level — choosing strategies, not moves.

### What Dies When You Only Follow Scripts

A cuber who ONLY runs scripts without ever thinking is no longer solving. They're executing. The scripts have become rigid — they work for known patterns but fail on novel states.

**The same failure mode exists in every domain:**
- The programmer who only applies design patterns without understanding the problem
- The doctor who only follows diagnostic flowcharts without clinical judgment
- The manager who only runs meeting templates without reading the room

They have vocabulary without grammar. Scripts without thinking. The snap fires but the mind isn't freed — it's bypassed.

### The Principle

**Knowledge is when to think and planning scripts that free the mind.**

Not the scripts themselves — those are just tools. Not the vocabulary — that's just compression. The intelligence is:

1. **Building** scripts that handle the routine (snap to known → execute)
2. **Knowing** when the current situation is routine vs novel (snap vs delta)
3. **Planning** with the freed cognition (compose scripts into strategy)
4. **Recognizing** when a script needs updating (the feel is wrong — new delta)

This is the cycle:

```
Experience → Pattern recognition → Script creation → Script automation
     ↑                                                    ↓
Delta detected ← Script failure ← Novel situation ← Script execution
```

The mind oscillates between:
- **Building scripts** (thinking, attention-heavy, slow)
- **Running scripts** (automatic, attention-free, fast)
- **Monitoring for deltas** (light attention, checking if snap still works)
- **Rebuilding when deltas accumulate** (back to thinking)

### What This Means For AI Architecture

Current AI: all thinking, no scripts. Every token is computed fresh. No snap, no compression, no freed cognition planning ahead.

What AI should be:
1. **Snap incoming context to known patterns** — "I've seen this structure before, I have a script"
2. **Execute the script automatically** — don't recompute what's known
3. **Monitor for deltas** — is the situation actually what the script expects?
4. **Use freed cognition to plan ahead** — compose scripts, anticipate novel states, build new scripts
5. **Know when to think vs when to run** — the meta-snap

The Rubik's cube metaphor gives us the architecture:
- **Moves** = primitive operations (token generation)
- **Algorithms** = compressed scripts (snap-matched sequences)
- **Grammar** = composition rules (which scripts chain)
- **Strategy** = planning with freed cognition (the real intelligence)

**Efficiency isn't about fewer moves. It's about scripts that free the mind to plan.** The 150-move solve beats the 20-move solve not because it's shorter, but because the 150 moves are composed of scripts the mind can manage while planning ahead.
