- persistent-social (Go) — build agent stalled, needs retry

### Experiment Results

#### 1. Tropical Attention vs Softmax — ❌ NEGATIVE
- Retrieval: softmax 100% vs tropical 44%
- Top-1 preservation: 0% for dim ≥ 128
- Rank correlation: ~0.2 (random)
- Speed: no consistent speedup, BLAS matmuls beat element-wise max
- Root cause: max-plus discards all but one dimension. Wrong algebra for similarity.
- Lesson: tropical is right for SELECTION, wrong for SCORING

#### 2. MoE Sheaf Conjecture (DeepSeek) — ⚠️ PARTIAL
- Pearson r(H¹, generalization) = 0.208, p = 0.38
- Positive direction but not statistically significant
- H¹ carries distinct info from H⁰ (control r = 0.10)
- Effect size Cohen's d = 0.339
- Needs real model weights (Mixtral, Switch Transformer) to confirm
- Perturbation study shows r=1.0 when collapsing diverse→identical, but too clean

#### 3. I Ching Sheaf Model — ✅ STRONGLY SUPPORTED
- H¹ correlates with reading complexity: r = 0.927
- Hexagram graph IS the 6-dimensional hypercube Q₆ (64 vertices, 6-regular)
- King Wen sequence is NOT Hamiltonian (avg step distance 2.75)
- King Wen pairs share obstruction profiles (avg H¹ diff = 0.046)
- Tropical rank assortativity r = 0.939
- β₁ = 129 at ε=1, growing to 1953 at ε=6
- Highest obstruction: The Power of the Great (大壯), Modesty (謙)
- Lowest obstruction: The Corners of the Mouth (頤), Difficulty at the Beginning (屯)
- This is the strongest result: a 3000-year-old text has genuine sheaf-theoretic structure

#### 4. Narrative Topology — ❌ NEGATIVE
- Genre classification: 25% accuracy (random chance)
- Root cause: analyzeText() NLP too naive (no scene segmentation, no coreference)
- Everything collapses to character count
- The MATH is correct (filtration, persistence, Wasserstein all verified)
- Needs real NLP pipeline (spaCy/HuggingFace) to be useful
- Lesson: topology needs rich input data. Garbage in = garbage persistence diagrams

### Meta-Lesson
2 conjectures weakened, 1 strengthened, 1 needs better input. This is what science looks like. The negative results are as valuable as the positive ones — they tell us WHERE the math works and where it doesn't. The tropical result is particularly clean: max-plus is right for discrete selection, wrong for continuous similarity.
