## The Nemotron Resolution on δ/20 vs 24%

Nemotron cracked the discrepancy that the other 4 models missed:

- **δ/20 = 5%**: Error relative to MAXIMUM POSSIBLE deflection (theoretical δ)
- **24%**: Error relative to ACTUAL Euler-Bernoulli deflection (which is δ/4 for uniform load)
- **Both are correct** — they're measuring against different baselines

For engineering: the 24% matters (that's what you compare against the actual beam).
For mathematics: the 5% matters (that's the pointwise bound).
For production: **neither matters** because both are within engineering tolerance for shipwright work.

**Nemotron's verdict**: "The only thing that's actually true: the experiments pass. The method works in practice."
