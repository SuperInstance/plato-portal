# Product Principles — The North Star

*Distilled from a night of fishing, jazz, drums, and calculus.*

## 1. The Depth Sounder You Stop Looking At
Tools succeed when they're fully internalized. The fisherman who knows the bottom by heart doesn't check the screen. The musician who knows the theory by heart doesn't think about scales. Our tools should be the bathymetrics you eventually outgrow — because they're IN you now.

## 2. The Engineer The Artist Forgets About
The best monitor mix is the one the musician never mentions. Our constraint math should be invisible during performance. Present when needed, gone when not. Supportive during struggle, silent during flow.

## 3. Two Fathoms From The Bottom
Better derivatives = closer to optimal. Every order of constraint resolution (position → velocity → acceleration → structure) lets you ride closer to the creative edge without crashing. The fish are always near the bottom. The best music is always near the edge.

## 4. The Vector You're Following
Constraints don't restrict — they carry. The beat is a river, not a wall. The chord changes are banks that give the river direction. When you're in flow, you don't make decisions — you follow the momentum of the constraint trajectory.

## 5. Mission Creep From Comfort
Grow because it works, not because you can. The captain asks for more hardware because voice steering is so natural they want it everywhere. Not because you pitched them features. Ship one thing that works so well they're comfortable. Then add.

## 6. Seven Modes of Engagement
Every musician has a different relationship to constraints:
- **Parker** — build the lattice through practice
- **Miles** — explore the frontier, always new
- **Ellington** — architect for emergence
- **Basie** — find consensus in real time
- **Goodman** — diagnose what you're missing
- **Armstrong** — liberate through constraint removal
- **Ella** — the tool disappears entirely

## 7. Bathymetrics for Music
The product is RESOLUTION, not restriction. We don't constrain music — we reveal its structure so clearly that musicians can ride closer to the creative bottom with confidence. We're the high-res chart that lets you fish at 2 fathoms instead of 8.

## The Success Metric

They stopped using the alternative and they don't reach for the backup buttons.
