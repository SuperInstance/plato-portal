## 1. "Obvious in Hindsight" Features

### 1a. **Constraint Whisper Mode — "Play something that ALMOST works"**
Right now the system enforces constraints (rigidity, lattice snap, etc.). But the magical zone in music is *near-failure* — the groove that's almost falling apart, the harmony that's one voice-leading step from resolved. **Inverse constraints**: instead of "satisfy all," do "satisfy N-1" and show you which one is *about* to break. Musicians would kill for a tool that says "your rhythm is rigid-compliant but the holonomy is drifting — lean into that." Tension is just unmet constraints.

### 1b. **The "Undo to Different Genre" Button**
Record a melody. The system stores the constraint-satisfaction path. Now "undo" but with different constraint weights — the same notes, re-snapped through a different lattice or deadband, and suddenly your folk tune is a prog-metal riff, or your jazz line is a gamelan pattern. Same DNA, different phenotype. The "genre" is just a constraint profile.

### 1c. **Time-Stretch That Preserves Rigidity**
When you time-stretch audio, groove dies. But if you know the Laman-rigid structure of the rhythm, you can stretch while *preserving the rigid subgraph* — stretching the "floppy" parts while keeping the structural beats locked. Nobody does this. It would sound insane — tempo changes that feel like the same groove, not a warped version.

### 1d. **Constraint-Based Auto-Arrangement for Beginners**
Give a beginner 3 notes they like. The system builds a Laman graph around those notes and generates the minimum viable accompaniment — the smallest rigid structure that makes those 3 notes sound *complete*. "You played these 3 notes. Here's the invisible scaffolding that makes them a song." Teaching music theory as structural engineering.

## 2. Cross-Domain Theft

### 2a. **From Cryptography: Zero-Knowledge Proofs for Musical Identity**
A ZKP lets you prove you know something without revealing it. What if you could prove "I composed this" or "this melody satisfies these constraints" without revealing the melody itself? Provably unique music fingerprints. You could verify a song meets a genre's constraint profile without hearing it. Weird, but think: royalty verification, plagiarism detection, compositional NFTs that are actually meaningful.

### 2b. **From Game Design: Fog of War → "Fog of Constraints"**
In strategy games, you can't see the whole map. What if composers worked in "fog" — they set constraints, but don't hear the result until they've committed? The reveal is the performance. Constraint-setting as a game. Multiplayer: two composers set conflicting constraints and the system finds the equilibrium. **Musical poker.**

### 2c. **From Mycology: Rhizome Composition**
Fungi grow as networks, not trees. A rhizome has no center, no hierarchy. What if your composition tool modeled songs as rhizomes — every bar, every motif is a node, and the "song" is any traversal of the network? The constraint system ensures every path is musically valid (rigid subgraphs guarantee coherence), but there's no fixed "song" — just a living network the performer navigates. Jazz musicians already think this way. Formalize it.

### 2d. **From Urban Planning: Zoning Laws for Harmony**
Cities have zoning: residential here, industrial there, mixed-use in between. Apply this to frequency space and time. "Zone" your song: bass frequencies are industrial (strict constraints, heavy regulation), midrange is commercial (flexible but guidelines), treble is residential (free-form, creative). The constraint primitives become zoning ordinances. You can "rezone" sections. Variances require approval (the system checks structural integrity). Suburban drones, downtown bangers.

### 2e. **From Cooking: Maillard Reactions as Harmonic Browning**
The Maillard reaction is that thing where heat + sugar + protein = flavor complexity. It's emergent — you don't "make" it, you create conditions for it. What's the musical equivalent? Certain constraint combinations that, when they interact, produce emergent complexity that neither produces alone. Map the "Maillard zones" in your constraint space — the combinations where rigidity + holonomy + lattice snap create harmonics that aren't in any of the inputs. Then make those the featured instrument.

Your constraint system could generate *personalized rhythmic scaffolding* — deadband funnels tuned to a patient's motor variability, metronome constraints that adapt to their recovery trajectory. ### 3c. **Negotiators and Mediators**

They don't care about music; they care about beautiful math with output.

### 3e. **Dog Trainers and Animal Behaviorists**
