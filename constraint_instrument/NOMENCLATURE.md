# NOMENCLATURE.md — The Naming System

> *Every word on this instrument means something. Not `parameter_3` — SAUDADE.
> Not `consensus_score` — CHORUS. The names teach.*

---

## Philosophy

English is the functional default (API names, class names). But the EXPERIENTIAL names — the ones musicians see and feel — draw from every language that has a word for something English can't express. A polyglot approach to sound.

Three layers exist for every concept:

1. **API Name** — English, `snake_case`, what developers type
2. **Panel Name** — what the musician sees, drawn from world languages
3. **Description** — the etymology, the feeling, the story

---

## The Five Primitives

The fundamental dials of the constraint instrument. Every mode uses some combination of these five forces.

| # | API Name | Panel Name | Origin | Meaning |
|---|----------|------------|--------|---------|
| 1 | `lattice_snap` | **MOORING** | English (nautical) | Where you tie up. The anchor point that keeps you tethered to the constraint surface. Like a boat at mooring — you can drift, but you know where home is. |
| 2 | `funnel` | **SAUDADE** | Portuguese | A longing for something not yet reached. The gravitational pull toward certain pitches — not a rule, but a yearning. The funnel doesn't force; it aches. |
| 3 | `is_laman` | **RIGGING** | English (nautical) | Is the structure holding? Are the lines taut? Laman's theorem says the lattice is rigid — RIGGING asks: are the constraint lines properly rigged, or are some slack? |
| 4 | `consensus` | **CHORUS** | Greek *χορός* (khorós) | The dance together. When multiple constraint voices agree, they form a chorus — not unison, but consensus. The word literally means "circular dance." |
| 5 | `holonomy` | **GEIST** | German | The spirit of the loop. Holonomy measures how much you've changed after completing a cycle. Geist — spirit, ghost, mind — captures the uncanny return: you're back where you started, but transformed. |

## The Seven Modes

Each mode is a complete approach to performing with constraints, named for the musician who embodied it.

| # | API Name | Panel Name | Origin | Meaning |
|---|----------|------------|--------|---------|
| 1 | `parker` | **SITZFLEISCH** | German | "Sitting flesh" — the stamina of practice. Parker internalized every change so deeply that the constraints disappeared. This mode is about building that muscle memory through relentless, focused repetition. |
| 2 | `miles` | **WANDERLUST** | German | The compulsion to explore, always seeking new territory. Miles Davis never repeated himself; every performance went somewhere it hadn't been. This mode pushes toward the frontier. |
| 3 | `ellington` | **ARCHITETTURA** | Italian | The architecture — building structures for others to inhabit. Ellington composed for his band, his soloists, his audience. This mode creates frameworks; it builds houses, not just rooms. |
| 4 | `basie` | **SAISON** | French | The season — finding rhythm together as it emerges. Basie's band swung as one organism. This mode doesn't impose a groove; it discovers the shared pulse through listening. |
| 5 | `goodman` | **DIAGNOSTICO** | Italian | Clinical precision — knowing exactly what's missing. Goodman was a perfectionist who could diagnose a band's weakness in one bar. This mode doesn't play; it listens and tells you what's absent. |
| 6 | `armstrong` | **LIBERTAD** | Spanish | Liberation through constraint removal. Armstrong stripped away everything that didn't serve the music. This mode starts loaded with constraints and frees you by removing them one by one. |
| 7 | `ella` | **DUENDE** | Spanish (Andalusian) | The dark fire — when the tool disappears and only music remains. Federico García Lorca described duende as the mysterious force that makes great art. Ella's scat singing had it: pure flow, no thinking, just music. |

## The Monitor States

The invisible engineer's instruments — what the monitor reads and reports.

| API Name | Panel Name | Origin | Meaning |
|----------|------------|--------|---------|
| `flow_state` | **DUENDE LEVEL** | Spanish | How much dark fire is present. 0% = mechanical, 100% = the instrument has vanished. |
| `error_rate` | **STRAY** | English | How far from home. Not "wrong" — just stray. A stray dog isn't broken, it's just wandered. |
| `consistency` | **GROOVE DEPTH** | English | How deep the pocket. Consistency isn't boring — it's the depth of the groove, how locked-in the rhythm is. |
| `exploration_velocity` | **HORIZON** | English | How far you can see. Exploration isn't randomness — it's extending the horizon of what's possible. |
| `energy` | **FIRE** | English | Raw intensity — average velocity, how hard you're pushing. |
| `energy_trajectory` | **EMBER** | English | Is the fire growing or fading? The trajectory of energy over time. |
| `is_vanished` | **MA** | Japanese 間 | The space where the tool doesn't exist. Ma (間) is the Japanese concept of "negative space" or "the gap" — when the monitor has detected full internalization and vanishes into silence. |

## Terrain Properties

The dimensions of a musical landscape — the bathymetric chart.

| API Name | Panel Name | Origin | Meaning |
|----------|------------|--------|---------|
| `scale_degrees` | **SEEDS** | English | What can grow here. Scale degrees aren't rules — they're seeds. Some grow strong (high weight), some struggle (low weight), some are the wild blue notes that grow between the cracks. |
| `funnel_targets` | **GRAVITY WELLS** | Physics | Where things fall toward. The gravitational centers of the terrain — pitches that pull you in, whether you intend to land on them or not. |
| `register_tendency` | **TERRAIN** | French (terre) | The ground you walk on. Register tendency defines the physical space — low valleys, high ridges — where this music naturally lives. |
| `chromatic_density` | **SPICE** | English | How much chromatic flavor. 0% = pure diatonic water, 100% = everything is available, total chromatic saturation. |
| `typical_tempo` | **PULSE** | English | The heartbeat. Not a metronome — a pulse. Every terrain has a natural resting heart rate. |
| `characteristic_intervals` | **FINGERPRINTS** | English | The intervals that define this terrain's sonic identity. Like a fingerprint — unique, recognizable, inescapable. |
| `rhythmic_skeletons` | **BONES** | English | The rhythmic skeleton underneath the flesh of the music. The bones that hold it up. |
| `rigidity` | **ICE** | English | How frozen are the rules. 0% = liquid, anything flows. 100% = rigid, no escape from the lattice. |
| `blues_notes` | **CRACKS** | English | The places where the lattice doesn't quite hold. Blue notes don't sit on the grid — they live in the cracks between scale degrees. |

## The Terrains

Musical landscapes, each a complete world. Named in the language of their origin.

| API Name | Panel Name | Origin | Etymology |
|----------|------------|--------|-----------|
| `delta_blues` | **BLUES DELTA** | Spanish word order | "The delta of sorrows." The Spanish inversion (noun then qualifier) mirrors how delta blues builds: the feeling first, the form second. |
| `bebop` / `bebop_rich` | **BEBOP** | Onomatopoeia | The sound itself. Bebop is named for the scat syllables — it doesn't translate, it IS. The word is the music. |
| `modal_jazz` | **RAGA** | Sanskrit राग (rāga) | Color, mood, passion. Modal jazz's closest ancestor. A raga isn't a scale — it's a complete melodic framework with rules for ascent, descent, emphasis, and time of day. Modal jazz is its American cousin. |
| `classical_counterpoint` | **KONTRAPUNKT** | German | The original term. Counterpoint entered English from the Latin *punctus contra punctum* (note against note), but the German preserves the discipline's architectural weight. |
| `bluegrass` | **HIGHLONESOME** | Appalachian English | The feeling, not the genre. "High lonesome sound" is what Bill Monroe called it — that keeling, mountain-top ache. Bluegrass is named for the grass; HIGHLONESOME is named for what it feels like. |
| `hip_hop_trap` | **808** | English (tech) | The Roland TR-808 drum machine that IS the genre. The 808 kick is so fundamental to trap that naming this terrain anything else would be dishonest. |
| `afro_cuban` | **CLAVE** | Spanish | The key — literally. La clave is both the instrument (two wooden sticks) and the rhythmic pattern that unlocks all Afro-Cuban music. Everything else is commentary. |
| `indian_raga` | **SRUTI** | Sanskrit स्रुति | The 22 microtones of the Indian musical system. Where Western music has 12 pitches, Indian music recognizes 22 places between them. SRUTI is the resolution that hears what Western ears can't. |
| `chinese_silk_bamboo` | **SĪZHU** | Mandarin 絲竹 | Silk and bamboo — the instruments. 絲 (silk = string instruments) and 竹 (bamboo = wind instruments). The name IS the ensemble. |
| `electronic_techno` | **KICK** | English | The heartbeat of techno. Not a bass drum — THE kick. The four-on-the-floor pulse that defines an entire culture of music. Everything in techno exists in relation to the kick. |
| `gospel` | **TESTIFY** | English | The act, not the genre. Gospel music isn't a style — it's testimony. "Testifying" is what happens when the music crosses from performance into witness. |
| `free_improvisation` | **MA** | Japanese 間 | The space where anything can happen. Ma (間) — the same concept as the monitor's vanished state — but here it's the terrain itself. Free improvisation is the music of ma: the space between notes IS the music. |

## Usage

### In Python

```python
from constraint_instrument.nomenclature import display_name, full_description

print(display_name("funnel"))           # → "SAUDADE"
print(display_name("miles"))            # → "WANDERLUST"
print(display_name("delta_blues"))      # → "BLUES DELTA"
print(display_name("flow_state"))       # → "DUENDE LEVEL"

# Full descriptions with etymology
print(full_description("holonomy"))     # → "GEIST (German) — The spirit of the loop..."
```

### In the CLI

```bash
# Terrains show panel names
python -m constraint_instrument terrains

# Diagnose uses panel names in the report
python -m constraint_instrument diagnose --mode parker --terrain delta_blues
```

### Multi-language support

```python
from constraint_instrument.nomenclature import display_name

# Default is English panel names
display_name("funnel")                          # → "SAUDADE"
display_name("funnel", lang="de")               # → "SEHNSUCHT" (German equivalent)
display_name("funnel", lang="ja")               # → "憧れ" (Japanese equivalent)
```
